Now, we will provide you with information about the outcomes of the three competitions between the teams of {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.group }}s and {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.outgroup }}s. For all {{C.NUM_TASKS_W}} tasks, you will receive information indicating which team won the competition, i.e., performed better on that task (or, in case of a tie, won the coin flip).

The comparison will be shown using a ">" or "<" sign:

  • ">" indicates that the team on the left won the competition.
  • "<" indicates that the team on the right won the competition.

Additionally, the team that won the competition will be highlighted in green.

Each of the three pieces of information you receive can be either true or false. This depends on the source of the information:

  • In {{C.SIGNAL_ACC_FQ}} of {{C.SIGNAL_TOT_FQ}} cases, a piece of information comes from the "True News Source" and is correct.
  • In {{C.SIGNAL_WRONG_FQ}} of {{C.SIGNAL_TOT_FQ}} cases, a piece of information comes from the "False News Source" and is incorrect, meaning it shows the opposite of what actually happened in the competition.

However, you do not know from which sources the information come.
