You will now receive information about the outcomes of the competitions between the {{ if participant.belief_ref == "ingroup" }} teams of {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.group }}s and {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.outgroup }}s. {{ else }} teams of {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.outgroup }}s and {{C.NUM_MEMBERS_W}} {{ participant.group }}s. {{ endif }} For each task, you will receive one piece of information indicating which team won the competition, i.e., performed better on that task (or won the coin flip in case of a tie). This information will be indicated by a ">" or "<" sign: ">" means that the team on the left won against the team on the right in the corresponding task, while "<" means the opposite. In addition, winning team's background color will be highlighted in green.

These hints will be given to you by creatures. There are 30 creatures of two different types:

Truth Tellers Liars

  • There are {{C.creatures_true}} Truth Tellers who always tell the truth about which team won the competition.
  • There are {{C.creatures_false}} Liars always lie about which team won the competition, so always tells the opposite of what actually happened.


For both tasks, one of the creatures will give you a hint about the outcome of the competition. Each creature is equally likely to provide you with the hint. Therefore, you are twice as likely to receive the hint from a Truth Teller as from a Liar, since there are twice as many Truth Tellers as Liars. However, you will not know which creature will give you the hint.

In other words, for each task, you will receive information indicating which team won the competition. This information is true in {{C.SIGNAL_ACC_FQ}} out of {{C.SIGNAL_TOT_FQ}} cases (i.e., it comes from one of the {{C.creatures_true}} Truth Tellers) and false in {{C.SIGNAL_WRONG_FQ}} out of {{C.SIGNAL_TOT_FQ}} cases (i.e., it comes from one of the {{C.creatures_false}} Liars).
